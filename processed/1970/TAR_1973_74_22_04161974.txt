Money Talk Money
The CMU Film Festival viewed for two evenings last weekend in a 
cramped. over-packed and smote-filled room may well have been CMU s
last breath ot the student film [art media.
The problem as usual is the pervading money first attitude.
The film festival is funded jointly by Bob McCurdys Discretionary 
Fund and the A.B.s Programming Fund. When the Film Festival presen
ted its budget request to the Programming Fund for this year, it failed to 
gain full support. Had it not been tor the additional money provided by 
McCurdy there wouldn't have been a Festival.
As of next year, that avenue for sponsoring activities that either 
Finance Committee or A B will not, will be defunct. A budgetary restric
tion has destined the Discretionary Fund and consequently the Film 
Festival to probable death
The Film Festival is the only outlet for film oriented students to 
show what theyve done, to get a wide range of critical feedback and to
experiment with an otherwise expensive art form. None of the arguments 
are stroma enough, apparently, without strona baekina. And since there is
no tilm department here,{were a little slow in the media area and the 
Festival brings no revenue to the university it doesnt have too many
places to turn for aid.
The elimination of the Discretionary Fund and the possible end of
the experimental Film Festival is just one more way of non-serving the 
students at Cranberry-Melon U where money is our business.
Christine Infante
