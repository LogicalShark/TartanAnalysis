For Want 
of Planning
David (j RoUSSOS Production Head: Vivian Kerstein
Acting Editor-itl-Chief Special Assistance: Amy Boyle, Judy Prober, Don Simon, Mike Fryd
With September comes many things. But too often the 
usual happenings one gets involved with at the end of sum
mer, are too much habit and not enough thought. Unfor
tunately, the conscious effort to plan ahead which is required 
of most college students is abandoned when these same 
people step away from the academics of school.
For the past two years, various irregularities have caused 
Student Government Presidential elections to be successfully 
appeal led. In 1976, it was found that some of the polls were 
dosed at hours designated for voting. In 1977, one of the 
Presidential candidates was found ineligible as a candidate as 
he was not a registered student. Both years somebody within 
the group faulted and this went wrong. The boat that started 
rocking eventually tipped over because no one know how to 
prevent it. In both instances, elections har' to be held a 
second time for a president to be successfully elected. Then, 
in both instances, the second election was run smoothly and 
efficiently.
During the summer, it was discovered that the TARTANs 
newly elected Editor-inChief had not been an enrolled 
student at Carnegie-Mellon University since the spring semes
ter of 1975. Someone slipped up here, as well. No one 
thought to check if the new E-ift-C was a CMU student. 
Yet responsibilities were assumed and the paper is still 
working.. with its first issue out earlier than any recent year 
and with new elections for Editor-in-Chief on September 14.
The Senate and the TARTAN had problems last semester, 
but both are still going strong. This is because somewhere at 
sometime it was realized that no organization can function 
without running into difficulties. This has to be allowed for. 
This has to be planned for.
Most groups on campus don t have an established order or 
system to work from - and this means more than just a one 
page consitution with quotes from Roberts Rules of Order.
It is true that in many cases this argument is invalid. Those 
cases are where the group is small and very specific in its 
objective. The Chess Club and Volleyball Club are interested 
in pursuing their respective sports. This makes for a simple 
and easy existence since it is rarely very difficult for someone 
to get together a chess or volleyball game.
But with the Carnegie-Mellon University radio station, 
WRCT, the situation is more complex. Since last May, WRCT 
has lost most of its Executive Board. The constitution lists no 
chain of command, so anyone still with the station has no 
authority to take steps to correct the situation. Student 
Senates Finance Committee Chairperson has frozen their 
budget.
There is no reason why an organization which has a budget 
from Senate of over $8,000, an interested membership 
involved with a unique organization, and the extensively 
equipped facilities that WRCT has should have any problem 
in policing themselves. Yet, that is what has happened and 
the radio station will not be on the air when classes begin.
This is where the stations problems begin to snowball. The 
longer WRCT is off the air, the greater the danger grows that 
it will lose its FM frequency. The longer WRCT remains 
inoperable as an orgaization, the more they will lose in 
present and future membership. And if WRCT remains 
unable to prove themselves competent as an organization, 
there exists the chance that their funds will be lost.
Because WRCT had not made the proper plans, their 
future is in question. A situation which could never happen 
 has happened.

Carnegie-Mellon track and cross country 
coach Gary Meckley has landed one of the 
nations top running prospects in five-time 
junior college All-American Robin Holland 
Holland will transfer to CMU this fall 
from the Community Colleg of Allegheny 
#County, Northside Campus, where he won 
three junor college national championships 
under CCAC track coach Neil Cohen.
Holland was recruited by everyone in 
the country With a distance running pro
gram, according to Cohen, including track 
powers Penn, Villanova,Oregon, Ohio State 
Texas-El Paso, Fordham, Washington State, 
Pitt, Penn State and Minnesota.
All Academic All-American with a 3.5 
average in Computer Science, Holland 
chose CMU because of its computer com
plex, which is the largest on any campus in 
the nation.
Robin set a National Junior College 
record when he won the marathon. title in 
1976 with a time of two hours, 23 minutes
56 seconds. He also won titles in cross 
country and indoor two-mile in 1976.
He qualified as an All-American twice in 
cross country, once in the marathon and in 
the indoor two-mile and outdoor 5,000 
meter events. ?
Robins potential is unlimited if he 
sticks to running faithfully, said Cohen. 
He could be the Frank Shorter of the 
future. -\
Weve had some great kids in our athle
tic program (at ACAC), Cohen said, but 
we never had anyone who wrapped it up so 
well as a student, athlete, person and 
all-american.
Meckley, whose 10-1 cross country team 
was the top-ranked NCAA Div.III team in 
Pennsylvania last fall, agrees that the South 
African native has a bright future.
Certainly, hes got the potential to be a 
national champion at some stage, said 
Meckley, and hes the type of student 
were always looking for.
This citys football fanatics will have a 
chance to quench their amazing appetites 
with The First Oakland Doubleheader 
football card on Saturday, Sept. 10 when 
Carnegie-Mellon plays California State 
prior to the Pitt-Notre Dame game.
The Tartans, as usual, will be playing at 
Tech Field on the Carnegie-Mellon campus. 
But CMU athletic director James Banner 
and Cal State AD Roy Terry have agreed to 
move up the starting time of the TartanVulcan game from 1:30p.m. to 12:30 p.m. 
so that interested fans can get double the
football action.
After the game, there will be plenty of 
time to take the short walk to Pitt Stadium 
to see the defending national champion 
Panthers take on their No. 1 challenger, the 
Fighting Irish.
Theres ample parking on the CMU 
campus, with buses running regularly 
outside the stadium, so taking in the 
Tartans season opener could be a good 
way of beating the late afternoon rush in 
lower Oakland.
Klein, Kepple Named Coaches
Athletic Director James Banner has 
named Gene Klein as the new Tartan 
varsity soccer coach and Janet Kepple as 
the new womens basketball coach for the 
1977-78 season.
Ms. Kepple has had an outstanding career 
at Shaler High School, winning 76 games 
and losing only 14 over the past five sea
sons with one section title coming in 1974. 
She also coached Shalers girls softball 
team to a section crown this year.
Ms. Kepple is a native of Johnstown,Pa., 
and a graduate of Indiana U. of Pennsyl
vania, where she played basketball and vol
leyball.
Klein, 25, of Bridgeville, was a varsity 
assistant soccer coach at Bethel Park High 
School last season, and has been a goalie on 
the Beadling Amateur Soccer Club of 
Upper St. Clair for the past six years. He is 
a graduate of the Univeristy of Pittsburgh.
