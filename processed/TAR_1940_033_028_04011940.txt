THE CARNEGIE TARTANMONDAY, APRIL 1, 1940Student Discovers Bad 
Situation On Campus
With general elections coining to tlie Carnegie campus let
ters have been pouring into the Tartan office from students who 
advance their views on everything from possible reforms in stu
dent government to their suggestions as to the person who 
should become the next President of Student Council. The 
naivete of some of these letters is amazing, yet some advance 
sound ideas. The following letter is typical:
Dear Editor:
I am a senior, having duly and properly passed through 
the first three years of my college life at this very institu
tion. It accidentally came to my attention last week that 
the fees that I pay during registration are handled in part 
by that organization known as Student Council. Of course, 
being a commuter I have never really been interested in all 
these councils and lionoraries and things.
I asked some of the fellows in my department about it 
and we started a bull session. I never used to like bull ses
sions because there was always one radical who spoiled 
things by asking everybody questions, but this time I actu
ally found something interesting that I am sure all Carnegie 
students would like to know about.
There is a terrible situation on this campus that should 
be corrected during the next election, which I found out
 takes place every year about this time. I used to wonder 
about those people cluttering up the halls and asking me to 
vote for Frater So and So, who was a good guy, but 
now the entire story fits together. It seems that a number of 
years ago some fraternities banded together and. monopo
lized the elections, and have kept control since then, and 
keep certain officers year after year. In other words, you 
cant become a member of that Student Council group 
unless you are a member of one of those fraternities.
Now I dont think that that is at all fair! In fact I am 
sure that if all of the students knew about it they would 
immediately band together and do something about it. 
After all, that isnt democracy.
I think that the Tartan should really carry on from 
this point, and suggest some way out of this mess.
Sincerely yours,
JOHN LEEDS, PH & V 40.
We chose this letter because of its buoyant, hopeful spirit. 
Here, we said, is the kind of student, the kind of American, 
who blazes frontiers, and starts reform movements. And we 
will, indeed, carry on, Mr. Leeds.
The solution is as simple as the discovery of the problem. 
Since these fraternities have been in control of student govern
ment for such a long time, and since they have enjoyed so many 
honors, they would probably be more than willing to allow other 
people to find out how it feels to enjoy the same. Further, 
pimply winning elections year after year must become boring. 
It would be much more fun to feel that they have some competi
tion. So, we propose that for this year the fraternities form a 
No Candidate combine, and mass all their votes for dormi
tory and commuter nominees. _______/
