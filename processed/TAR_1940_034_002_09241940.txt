CARNEGIE TO CONSIDER DEBATE BID
Council May Invite 
Three Vice-Presidential 
Candidates to Clash
A proposal that Carnegie Tech act as host to the vice presi
dential candidates of the Democratic, Republican, and Socialist 
parties will be presented to Student Council tonight.
under the plan Henry "Wallace, Charles McNary, and May
nard Krueger would be invited to Carnegie to participate in a 
- symposium discussing the issues of the present campaign before 
an audience of Tech students. If the Student Council ap.proves 
the plan, it will be sent to the Executive Board on Thursday.
Sending invitations and handling general arrangements 
would probably be placed in the hands of the Studcnt-Faculty 
Committee on assemblies.
Definite word on whether such a program will be undertaken 
will appear in the next issue of the TARTAN.

The TARTAN, says the editor, writes about things before 
they happen, not after they happen. We cant help wondering 
how the TARTAN can be sure that Thistle Ilall wont be Struck 
by a torpedo before the Welcome Freshman Dance, that concert 
rates for students wont be lowered instead of raised, that the 
girls of Alpha Chi Alpha wont massacre each other before the 
scheduled Tuesday dinner meeting.
Such clairvoyance on the part of a college newspaper staff 
is an indication of the changing character of the countrys young 
people. No longer have we the post-war uncertainty of the Flap
per Era. The popular songs no longer say I Wonder Whos 
Kissing Her Now. Instead, they say with morbid confidence, 
Ill Never Smile Again. (They also say Boog-It, but we 
arent sure what sort of trend that signifies.) Our generation 
doesnt need Boy Scouting, character building, vocational guid
ance. We know whats going to happen next week by the Fri
day deadline.
The hand that holds the purse stringsin this case the di
rector of school equipmenthas bought Eddie a new truck. In 
spite of the fact that Eddie is probably the most obligingand 
the most handsometruck driver driving for any college in the 
country, he has got us into a lot of trouble this past year. Ever 
since Scotch and Soda made firm friends of ushe used to let 
us ride down to the Music Hall sitting on the scenerywe have 
been waving gayly at green trucks that we thought Eddie was 
driving, but which turned out to be the vehicles of the Depart
ment of Sanitation. Many is the time weve just missed a lift 
on a sprinkler wagon, whose sinister chauffeur was ready to take 
us heaven knows where; many is the time weve put our glasses 
on just soon enough to avoid the friendly advances of the driver 
who wanted us to sit on a pile of old shoes, coffee grounds, and 
damp newspapers, and he would get us rapidly wherever we 
wanted to go. Eddies new truck is green, too; our eyesight is 
worse than ever. Where is all this going to end?
A further note on the phenomenal changes here at school 
the little things that sting us deeplya sad reminder of the 
swift passage of our college days. The Hut, always so com
fortably drab, is being painted a light color, somehow reminiscent 
of the dying Worlds Fair. A new studio theater has been built, 
.nestling in the outskirts of the Commons Buildingwithin smell
ing. distance but not within reach of the Beanery ice cream cones. 
And. the post office has moved around the corner, probably to 
indicate that Jim Farley is no longer boss. There is a line of 
eager dorm boys at the new window, asking for their letters; all 
that remains to remind us that the old site once housed the U. S. 
Mails is an ancient three-cent stamp we found on the floor. It 
was. uncancelled, but the stickum had been licked off.
The graduate students at Miller Hall are working on a 
system of buzzers to awaken them in the mornings. Each man 
will set his own buzzer for the time he wants to get up. At that 
time, the buzzer, which is under his bed, will buzz, theoretically 
waking him up. All this is to eliminate the annoyance of several 
independent alarm clocks going off at fifteen-minute intervals. 
Maybe its different when you have a degree, but it would take 
; mere than a buzzer buzzing under our bed to wake us up for an 
eight-thirty. This column suggests a slight electric shock, which 
will serve the purpose not only of awakening the sleeper, but 
; also of getting him out of bed. It would lighten many weary 
undergraduate hearts to see a graduate student on some frosty 
morning, his only covering a pair of striped pajamas, leaping out 
; of Miller Hall in a retarded state of electrocution.

'lifted, by order of the Department of Safety. The epidemic of 
distemper, they say, has abated. Director Fairley has neglected, 
however* to muzzle an afflicted Scottie we know. Why do we 
:pay taxes?
