New Senior Election System
There is before Student Council at the present time an 
amendment to the Tartan constitution which lias as its goal a 
broadening of votes so that control of the paper by any single 
group becomes virtually impossible. At; the present time the 
incoming staff is elected by a vote of the outgoing staff. In the 
new plan the following takes place:
(1) The old staff elects the new Senior Managing Board 
without enumerating positions. Bach member of the incoming 
and outgoing staffs is given one vote, except the editor, who is 
given two votes in case of a draw ballot.
(2) The outgoing and incoming boards then elect the new 
editor.
(!i) The new editor and the old board then elect the remain
der of the officers.
With this system the ballot is more democratic, the incom
ing staff has an opportunity to select its leader, and the new 
editor has a voice in apportioning the remainder of the positions.
Tests For Instructors
Since students must face an Examiner before being ad
mitted to Carnegie, and since the institution has recently be
come addicted to trail blazing in collegiate education, the Tartan 
humbly suggests that the administration adopt one more pio
neering policy.
We suggest that the administration organize a Personnel 
Department to prepare job, analyses, and give tests to determine 
intelligence, interest, competence, temperament, and degree of 
maturity, of all candidats for teaching positions at Carnegie. 
Sample questions for these tests follow:
On the temperament test one of the questions could be 
Would you swear at your classes under any circumstances? 
Also, Do you think you might be subject to fits of anger while 
teaching! On the degree of maturity test a good question 
could be: Would you sulk and fume in class if, in a two-hour 
period, one of your students finds that he MUST leave the room 
for a few minutes? On the intelligence test should appear: 
Do you think that you might ever be in a position where you 
would have to tell one of your students to write a quiz paper in j 
one syllable words so tliat you could understand him? On the 
competence test should appear: Would you ever give admit
tedly impossible assignments? or Would you go through an 
entire semester without giving your students one word of in
struction? On the interest test should appear: Do you think
that you, in teaching your course in ............, would ever have to
admit 1o your students that you don't give a d.... about the 
course that you are teaching?
We could suggest; many more questions, luit; after investiga
tion the Personnel Department could easily compose some of 
its own.
This idea will probably sweep the country, for who else has 
ever thought of selecting a college instructor on a scientific 
basis? _______________________
